About This Website

Soap Making 101 is a platform designed to teach you how to create beautiful, natural soaps from home. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced crafter, you'll find step-by-step tutorials and helpful tips here.

Soap Making Ingredients and Steps

Ingredients Needed:

  • 2.5 oz (70g) of Sodium Hydroxide (Lye)
  • 6.5 oz (180g) of Olive Oil
  • 6.5 oz (180g) of Coconut Oil
  • 3 oz (85g) of Sweet Almond Oil
  • 3.5 oz (100g) of Water (distilled water is recommended)
  • Essential oils for fragrance (optional, about 1 oz or 30ml)
  • Colorants (optional, like mica powder or herbs)
  • Soap mold

Steps to Follow:

  1. *Prepare Your Work Area*: Clean your workspace, gather your equipment, and make sure to wear safety gear (gloves and goggles) to protect from the lye.
  2. *Mix Lye and Water*: Slowly add the sodium hydroxide (lye) to the distilled water (never the other way around) in a heat-safe container. Stir carefully until the lye dissolves and the mixture heats up. Let it cool to around 110°F (43°C).

  3. *Melt the Oils*: In a separate container, combine the olive oil, coconut oil, and sweet almond oil. Gently heat the oils until they are fully melted and mixed.
  4. *Combine Lye and Oils*: Slowly pour the cooled lye mixture into the melted oils. Using an immersion blender, mix the combination until it reaches a "trace" — a consistency that resembles pudding.
  5. *Add Fragrance and Colorants (Optional)*: If desired, add essential oils and any colorants or herbs to your soap mixture and stir well.
  6. *Pour Into Mold*: Pour the soap mixture into your soap mold and smooth the top with a spatula.
  7. *Let Soap Set*: Cover the mold with a towel and let the soap sit for 24 to 48 hours to harden and set. Once set, remove the soap from the mold.
  8. *Cure the Soap*: Allow the soap to cure in a cool, dry area for 3 to 4 weeks. This allows the soap to fully harden and ensures a milder product for your skin.
  9. *Enjoy Your Homemade Soap*: After curing, your soap is ready to use and enjoy!
